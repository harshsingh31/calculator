Virat Kohli

The King of Cricket

Here's a time line of Virat Kohli's career:

  • 1998 - Started training at the West Delhi Cricket Academy.
  • 2002-03 - Played for Polly Umrigar Trophy match. Became the highest run-scorer for Delhi Under-15 team.
  • 2003-04 - Selected for the Delhi Under-17 team. Played for Vijay Merchant Trophy. Highest scorer with 470 runs.
  • 2004-05 - Played for Vijay Merchant Trophy in Delhi Under-17 team.
  • 2006 - List-A match debut in Ranji Trophy. Score 90+ runs against Karnataka after his father's demise. Selected in the Under-19 team.
  • 2007 - T20 debut (Under-19).
  • 2008 - Became U-19 World Cup team captain. Lifted the Under-19 World Cup. International ODI debut at 19 years of age.
  • 2009 - ICC Championship Trophy. First Man of the Match award. Maiden ODI century.
  • 2011 - World Cup Indian team debut. First Debut Indian batsman to score a century against Bangladesh.
  • 2012 - Vice-Captain for the Asia Cup. Highest individual score against Pakistan (183 runs in ODI).
  • 2014 - Indian Test Squad Captain.
  • 2017 - Indian Team Captain across all formats. Score 2818 runs in International Cricket. First-ever batsman to hit six double hundreds as the Captain.
  • 2018 - Rank 1 in the ICC Test Rankings (August). First Indian Captain to hit 3 successive centuries in ODIs. Fastest player to score 10,000 ODI runs.
  • 2021 - Step down from T20 & ODI captaincy.
  • 2022 - Step down from Test Captaincy (January).

“There are players who are in form and there’s Kohli who wins you matches again and again.”

-- Indian Cricketer Yuvraj Singh.
